# Chess Notation Guide

A visual guide to reading and writing chess moves.

---

## 1. The Board Coordinate System

Every square on the board has a unique name made of a **letter + number**.

- **Letters (aвҖ“h)** label the 8 columns, called **files**, from left to right from White's perspective
- **Numbers (1вҖ“8)** label the 8 rows, called **ranks**, from bottom to top from White's perspective

## 4. Capturing вҖ” the "x"

When a piece captures another, an **x** is placed between the piece and the destination:

```
Nxe5   вҶ’ Knight captures whatever is on e5
exd5   вҶ’ pawn on the e-file captures whatever is on d5
```

For pawn captures, the **file the pawn came from** is written first:

```
exd5   вҶ’ a pawn from the e-file captures on d5
```

## 6. Reading a Full Game (PGN Format)

Moves are listed in pairs вҖ” **White's move first, then Black's** вҖ” with a move number before each pair:

```
1. e4 e5   2. Nf3 Nc6   3. Bc4 Bc5
```

Reading this out loud:
- Move 1: White plays e4, Black plays e5
- Move 2: White plays Nf3, Black plays Nc6
- Move 3: White plays Bc4, Black plays Bc5

This is the **Italian Game** вҖ” one of the most common openings.

## 7. Clock Annotations (what you'll see in your chess.com games)

Your game files include clock times after each move in curly braces:

```
1. e4 {[%clk 0:09:56]} 1... e5 {[%clk 0:09:59]}
```

- `{[%clk 0:09:56]}` means the player had **9 minutes and 56 seconds** remaining after making that move
- The `1...` before Black's first move just means "move 1, Black to play" вҖ” the `...` indicates it's Black's turn
